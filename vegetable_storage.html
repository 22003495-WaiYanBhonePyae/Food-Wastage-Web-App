General Tips for Vegetable Storage

Check Regularly

Inspect stored vegetables for signs of spoilage, like wilting or soft spots, and remove any affected pieces.

Separate Ethylene-Producing Vegetables & Fruits

Some fruits and vegetables, like apples, bananas, and tomatoes, produce ethylene gas, which can cause other vegetables to ripen and spoil faster. Store them separately.

Use Airtight Containers for Cut Vegetables

When storing leftover cut vegetables, use airtight containers to preserve freshness and prevent them from drying out.

1. Proper Storage

  • Cool, Dry, Dark Places: Store root vegetables (potatoes, onions) in a pantry, away from light.
  • Refrigeration: Keep leafy greens, carrots, and cucumbers in the fridge. Use bags or containers with paper towels to absorb moisture.
  • Separate Ethylene-Producers: Keep apples, bananas, and tomatoes away from other produce to prevent spoiling.

2. Preserve Leftovers

  • Freezing: Freeze fruits and vegetables (blanch first if needed) in bags or containers to keep them longer.
  • Pickling: Pickling fruits and vegetables in vinegar or brine is a common way to preserve them, such as making kimchi.

Why Blanching is Important

  • Stops enzyme action to prevent spoilage.
  • Preserves color and texture.
  • Locks in nutrients.
  • Reduces freezer burn.

3. Wrapping Techniques

  • Plastic Wrap or Cling Film: Use for cucumbers, celery, or peppers to retain moisture.
  • Damp Paper Towels: Wrap leafy greens in damp paper towels and place in perforated plastic bags.
