Purchasing Behaviour

Food wastage is a significant concern in Singapore, with households contributing substantially to the issue. Young homeowners, in particular, may engage in over-purchasing and subsequently waste food due to various cultural, economic, and psychological factors.

Cultural Factors

  • Abundance Mentality: The cultural inclination to ensure an abundance of food during gatherings often leads to over-preparation and purchasing more than necessary, resulting in leftovers.
  • Social Status and Generosity: Providing ample food can be seen as a sign of generosity, prompting individuals to buy in excess to meet societal expectations.
  • Convenience Culture: The popularity of convenience foods frequently results in excessive packaging waste and uneaten portions being discarded.

Economic Factors

  • Bulk Purchasing: Promotional deals and perceived cost savings encourage buying in larger quantities, but without proper storage plans, these purchases often spoil.
  • Affluence: Increased disposable incomes make young homeowners less cautious about over-purchasing.
  • Cheap Food Prices: Subsidized or low-cost food reduces the perceived value, leading to wastefulness.

Psychological Factors

  • Impulse Buying: Marketing strategies trigger impulsive purchases, leading to unnecessary acquisitions that are discarded.
  • Overestimation of Needs: A lack of meal planning results in over-purchasing.
  • Cognitive Dissonance: Many individuals acknowledge the negative effects of food waste but continue wasteful behaviors due to conflicting beliefs and actions.

Solutions to Mitigate Food Wastage

  • Meal Planning: Encourage better planning to reduce overbuying.
  • Education on Food Storage: Teach proper storage techniques to prolong freshness.
  • Awareness Campaigns: Initiatives like "Love Your Food. Waste Less. Save More." aim to educate the public.
  • Encouraging Smaller Portions: Advocate for cooking and serving smaller portions to prevent leftovers.
  • Composting: Promote urban composting to reduce landfill burdens and support climate action.
